## Section B — Comprehension (10 marks)

> Read the passage and answer the questions.

> Forests cover one-third of all land on Earth, providing vital organic infrastructure for some of the planet's densest, most diverse collections of life. They support countless species as well as 1.6 billion human livelihoods, yet humans also tend to destroy them, having turned about 30 % of original forests into farms, ranches, and cities. Another 20 % has been degraded. We can replant trees, but new forests can take centuries to mature, while old-growth forests once cleared are virtually irreplaceable.

(i) How much of the Earth's land is covered by forests? **(2)**
(ii) How many human livelihoods depend on forests? **(2)**
(iii) What percentage of original forests have been turned into farms, ranches, and cities? **(2)**
(iv) Why are old-growth forests considered irreplaceable? **(2)**
(v) Suggest a suitable title for the passage. **(2)**

## Section D — Précis Writing (10 marks)

> Read the following passage carefully. Write its précis in about one-third of its original length and suggest a suitable title.

> Books are our best friends. They never deceive us, never demand anything from us, and never speak harshly. They give us company in our loneliness, knowledge in our ignorance, and pleasure in our distress. From a good book we can learn the experiences of great minds without travelling, the wisdom of ancient sages without conversation, and the lessons of history without witnessing it. Choosing the right book, however, is as important as choosing the right friend, for a bad book corrupts the heart while a good one elevates the soul. Therefore, every student must be trained to develop the habit of reading good books from an early age, for this single habit can shape the character and destiny of an individual more powerfully than any other influence. *(approx. 130 words)*
