# English (Compulsory) — HSSC Part I

KPTBB / BISE Abbottabad · 100 marks · 3 hours · No practical

## Paper pattern at a glance

| Section | Marks | Format |
|---|---|---|
| A — MCQs | 10 × 1 = 10 | Vocabulary, grammar, comprehension extract |
| B — Comprehension | 10 | Unseen passage with 5 questions |
| C — Translation (Urdu → English) | 10 | One paragraph |
| D — Précis writing | 10 | Reduce a paragraph to one-third |
| E — Letter / Application | 10 | Personal letter OR application |
| F — Story / Dialogue / Paragraph | 10 | Choose one |
| G — Textbook short questions | 20 | Modern Prose & Heroes / One-Act Plays / Poems — answer 4 of 6 short questions |
| H — Essay (extended writing) | 20 | Choose 1 of 4 topics, ~250–300 words |

Total: **100**.

> Some sessions allocate slightly different splits (e.g. translation 5 + précis 5). The model paper here uses the standard 10-section split.

## Top guess topics

- **Essay topics that recur:** "Pollution and its remedies", "Importance of Education", "A Visit to a Hill Station", "Allama Iqbal", "Quaid-e-Azam", "Discipline", "Internet — Boon or Bane".
- **Letter recurrents:** to brother congratulating on success; to friend describing a sports day / cricket match; application for sick leave / fee concession / character certificate.
- **Précis passages:** typically descriptive (nature, science, society) of ~150 words.
- **Translation:** moralistic/anecdotal Urdu paragraph (~80 words) about hard work, honesty, friendship, or patriotism.

## Common student mistakes

- **Tense shift mid-essay** — students start in past, slip to present.
- **Précis longer than the original** because they don't count words.
- **Letter format errors** — missing date, salutation, or signature placement.
- **Translating idioms literally** (e.g. "نوسر نولا" = he ran for life, NOT "he ran with nine fingers").
- **Comprehension answers in their own words too often** — examiner wants reference to the passage, but graded on accuracy.
- **Spelling and capitalization** — proper nouns must be capitalised; loss of marks adds up fast.
- **Quoting too long from a poem** — a 2-line reference is enough.

## Recommended study order

1. Practise translation + précis daily — these together = 20 marks.
2. Memorise letter/application format — score 10 marks reliably.
3. Master 5 essay topics word-perfect — guarantees the 20-mark essay.
4. Read all 15 short stories at least twice, write a 4-line summary each.
5. Read all plays + poems; memorise the central theme and 2 quotes per piece.
6. Drill 30 idioms + 30 prepositions for MCQs.
7. Revise 12 tenses with one example each.
8. Practise 5 unseen comprehension passages from past papers.

## Useful templates

### Application format

```
The Principal,
[School Name],
[City].

Subject: [Subject of application]

Sir,
[Body — 3 short paragraphs: introduce, explain, request]

Yours obediently,
[Name]
Class: ____
Roll No.: ____
Date: ____
```

### Letter format (informal)

```
[Address]
[Date]

My dear [Name],

[Body — 3 paragraphs: greetings, main message, closing wish]

Yours sincerely,
[Your name]
```

### Essay structure (5 paragraphs)

1. **Introduction** — define topic + thesis statement.
2. **Body 1** — first key point with example.
3. **Body 2** — second key point with example.
4. **Body 3** — third key point with example.
5. **Conclusion** — restate thesis + call to action.
