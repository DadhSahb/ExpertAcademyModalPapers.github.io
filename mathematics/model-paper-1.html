Section A — Multiple Choice Questions (20 marks)

Encircle the correct option. Each question carries 1 mark.

  1. The conjugate of (3 − 2i) is: (a) 3 + 2i (b) −3 − 2i (c) −3 + 2i (d) 2 − 3i

  2. If A is a square matrix and |A| = 0, then A is called: (a) Identity (b) Singular (c) Symmetric (d) Orthogonal

  3. The solution of 2x + 1 = 0 is: (a) −1/2 (b) 1/2 (c) −2 (d) 0

  4. Discriminant of ax² + bx + c = 0 is: (a) b² − 4ac (b) b² + 4ac (c) 4ac − b² (d) −b² − 4ac

  5. Number of terms in the expansion of (a + b)ⁿ is: (a) n (b) n − 1 (c) n + 1 (d) 2n

  6. The 10th term of the A.P. 2, 5, 8, … is: (a) 27 (b) 29 (c) 31 (d) 30

  7. The sum to infinity of the G.P. 1, 1/2, 1/4, … is: (a) 1 (b) 2 (c) 3/2 (d) 1/2

  8. n! equals: (a) n × (n−1)! (b) (n−1)! (c) n + (n−1)! (d) n × n

  9. Probability of getting a head when an unbiased coin is tossed: (a) 0 (b) 1 (c) 1/2 (d) 2/3

  10. C(n, r) = : (a) n!/(n−r)! (b) n!/(r!(n−r)!) (c) r!/(n!(n−r)!) (d) (n−r)!/r!

  11. One radian is equal to (approximately): (a) 45° (b) 60° (c) 57.3° (d) 90°

  12. sin 30° + cos 60° = : (a) 1 (b) 0 (c) 1/2 (d) √3/2

  13. cos(A − B) = : (a) cos A cos B − sin A sin B (b) cos A cos B + sin A sin B (c) sin A cos B + cos A sin B (d) sin A cos B − cos A sin B

  14. Period of tan x is: (a) π/2 (b) π (c) 2π (d) 4π

  15. In a triangle ABC, a/sin A = : (a) 2R (b) R (c) R/2 (d) 1

  16. Range of sin⁻¹ x is: (a) [0, π] (b) [−π/2, π/2] (c) [−π, π] (d) [0, 2π]

  17. The general solution of sin θ = 0 is: (a) θ = nπ (b) θ = (2n+1) π/2 (c) θ = 2nπ (d) θ = π/4

  18. If A ∪ B = A, then: (a) B ⊆ A (b) A ⊆ B (c) A = B (d) A ∩ B = ∅

  19. (1 + i)² = : (a) 0 (b) 2i (c) −2i (d) 2

  20. The middle term in the expansion of (a + b)⁸ is the: (a) 4th (b) 5th (c) 6th (d) 8th

Section B — Short Response Questions (40 marks)

Attempt any TEN (10) questions. Each question carries 4 marks.

  1. Find the multiplicative inverse of 3 − 4i and express the answer in a + bi form.
  2. Show that (A')' = A for any matrix A. Verify with a 2×2 example.
  3. Solve x⁴ − 5x² + 4 = 0.
  4. Resolve (7x + 3) / [(x + 1)(x − 2)] into partial fractions.
  5. Find the 12th term of an A.P. whose 4th term is 13 and 9th term is 28.
  6. Find the sum of the geometric series 2 + 6 + 18 + … + 4374.
  7. How many ways can the letters of the word EQUATION be arranged?
  8. A card is drawn from a well-shuffled deck of 52. Find P(king or queen).
  9. Use mathematical induction to prove 1 + 2 + 3 + … + n = n(n+1)/2 for n = 1, 2.
  10. Find the term independent of x in the expansion of (x + 1/x)¹⁰.
  11. Convert 225° into radian measure.
  12. Prove that sin(A + B) sin(A − B) = sin²A − sin²B.
  13. Solve the triangle in which a = 7 cm, b = 5 cm, C = 60°.
  14. Sketch the graph of y = 2 sin x for 0 ≤ x ≤ 2π. Mark the period and amplitude.
  15. Find the principal value of sin⁻¹(−1/2).
  16. Solve 2 cos² θ − 1 = 0 for 0 ≤ θ < 2π.
  17. Show that tan(45° + A) · tan(45° − A) = 1.
  18. Define a group. Show that (Z, +) is a group.
  19. Prove that nC(r) + nC(r−1) = (n+1)C(r).
  20. Find A⁻¹ if A = [[2, 1], [3, 4]].
  21. Verify De Morgan's law (A ∪ B)' = A' ∩ B' for A = {1, 2, 3}, B = {2, 4, 6}, universal U = {1..6}.

Section C — Long Response Questions (40 marks)

Attempt all FIVE (5) questions. Each carries 8 marks. Internal choice given.

Q22. (a) Solve the following system of equations using matrix inversion method: x + y + z = 6, 2x − y + z = 3, x + 2y − z = 2. (5) (b) Find the rank of the matrix [[1, 2, 3], [2, 4, 6], [3, 6, 9]]. (3)

OR

(a) Solve the system using Cramer's rule: 2x + 3y = 5, 4x − y = 1. (5) (b) Show that any matrix multiplied by its inverse gives the identity matrix, using a 2×2 example. (3)

Q23. (a) If the roots of the equation x² + px + q = 0 are α and β, find the equation whose roots are α/β and β/α. (5) (b) Find the value of k for which the equation kx² − 8x + 4 = 0 has equal roots. (3)

OR

(a) Solve √(2x + 3) + √(x + 1) = 5. (5) (b) Discuss the nature of roots of 3x² − 5x + 4 = 0. (3)

Q24. (a) The 5th and 9th terms of an A.P. are 11 and 23 respectively. Find the sum of the first 20 terms. (5) (b) Sum the geometric series 5 + 10 + 20 + … to 8 terms. (3)

OR

(a) Use mathematical induction to prove 1² + 2² + 3² + … + n² = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6. (5) (b) Find the middle term in the expansion of (2x − 1/x)⁶. (3)

Q25. (a) Prove that cos(A + B) cos(A − B) = cos²A − sin²B. (4) (b) Prove that sin 3A = 3 sin A − 4 sin³A. (4)

OR

(a) Prove that tan(A + B) = (tan A + tan B) / (1 − tan A tan B). (4) (b) Without using calculator, find the exact value of cos 75°. (4)

Q26. (a) In a triangle ABC, a = 13 cm, b = 14 cm, c = 15 cm. Find the area, in-radius r, and circumradius R. (5) (b) Solve tan x = √3 for 0 ≤ x ≤ 2π. (3)

OR

(a) From a point on a horizontal plane, the angle of elevation of the top of a tower 50 m high is 30°. Find the distance of the point from the foot of the tower. (5) (b) Find the principal value of cos⁻¹(−√3/2). (3)
