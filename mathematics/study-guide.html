High-weightage chapters carry roughly 55% of the paper. Mastering Ch 3, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 alone secures a solid pass; everything else is grade-improvement.

Top guess topics — almost-certain in every paper

  1. Solve a system of 3 linear equations using matrix inversion or Cramer's rule (LRQ).
  2. Resolve a fraction into partial fractions (SRQ, case 3 quadratic non-repeated is most common).
  3. Find the sum of n terms of an A.P. or G.P. given two facts about the sequence (LRQ).
  4. Prove a statement by mathematical induction (LRQ part-a).
  5. Find a specific term (e.g. middle, term independent of x) in a binomial expansion (LRQ part-b).
  6. Prove a sum/difference identity like sin(A+B) + sin(A−B) = 2 sin A cos B (LRQ).
  7. Solve a triangle (SAS or SSS) using law of cosines + law of sines (LRQ).
  8. One named formula MCQ every paper: nature of roots discriminant, period of cot x, value of sin 75°, etc.

Common student mistakes

  • Sign errors in expanding (a − b)ⁿ — students drop alternating signs.
  • Confusing A.M. with A.P. when only two means are inserted vs. n means.
  • Forgetting domain restriction when solving trig equations — losing half-marks for "general solution" missing.
  • Mis-applying Cramer's rule when the determinant is zero (system is inconsistent / dependent — they keep computing).
  • Using degrees inside calculus-style trig formulas — radian conversion is mandatory in Ch 9.
  • Skipping the verification step in induction (n = k+1 case) — examiners deduct 2 marks.

Formula sheet to memorize cold

  • Quadratic discriminant: Δ = b² − 4ac — nature of roots from sign of Δ.
  • Sum of roots α + β = −b/a; product αβ = c/a.
  • Sum of A.P. of n terms: Sₙ = n/2 · [2a + (n−1)d].
  • Sum of G.P. of n terms: Sₙ = a(1 − rⁿ)/(1 − r), sum to infinity (|r|<1): S∞ = a/(1 − r).
  • Binomial general term: Tᵣ₊₁ = C(n,r) aⁿ⁻ʳ bʳ.
  • sin(A+B) = sin A cos B + cos A sin B.
  • cos(A+B) = cos A cos B − sin A sin B.
  • Law of sines: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C = 2R.
  • Law of cosines: a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A.
  • Area of triangle: ½ ab sin C and s = (a+b+c)/2, Area = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)].
