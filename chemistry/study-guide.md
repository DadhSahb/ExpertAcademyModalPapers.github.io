## Common student mistakes

- **Mole-mass confusion** — students compute "moles of compound" using atomic mass instead of molar mass.
- **Wrong order of priority** in limiting-reagent problems; failing to convert all reactants to moles first.
- **Mixing up molarity (M) with molality (m)** — solvent kg vs solution L.
- **Drawing 4 lobes instead of 3 for sp² hybrid orbitals**, or vice versa.
- **Sign of ΔH wrong** — students write ΔH +ve for exothermic.
- **Forgetting the second Faraday's law** when comparing two electrolysis cells in series.
- **Confusing rate constant with reaction rate** — rate constant is independent of concentration.

## Formula sheet to memorize cold

- Mole: `n = mass / molar mass` = `N / Nₐ` where `Nₐ = 6.022 × 10²³`.
- Ideal gas: `PV = nRT`, `R = 0.0821 L·atm/mol·K = 8.314 J/mol·K`.
- van der Waals: `(P + an²/V²)(V − nb) = nRT`.
- Graham's: `r₁/r₂ = √(M₂/M₁)`.
- Bohr radius: `rₙ = 0.529 n² Å`. Energy: `Eₙ = −13.6/n² eV`.
- ΔH (Hess): sum equals net even via different paths.
- Equilibrium: `Kc = [products]^x / [reactants]^y`. `Kp = Kc (RT)^Δn`.
- Faraday: `m = (M·I·t) / (n·F)`, `F = 96500 C/mol`.
- Arrhenius: `k = A e^(−Eₐ/RT)`.
- Raoult's law: `Pₛₒₗᵤₜᵢₒₙ = X_solvent · P°_solvent`.
