Section A — Multiple Choice Questions (17 marks)

Encircle the correct option. Each carries 1 mark.

  1. The number of molecules in 22.4 L of any gas at STP is: (a) 6.022 × 10²² (b) 6.022 × 10²³ (c) 1.0 × 10²³ (d) 3.011 × 10²³

  2. The empirical formula of glucose C₆H₁₂O₆ is: (a) CHO (b) CH₂O (c) C₂H₂O (d) C₃H₆O

  3. Which gas diffuses fastest? (a) O₂ (b) N₂ (c) H₂ (d) CO₂

  4. STP corresponds to: (a) 25 °C, 1 atm (b) 0 °C, 1 atm (c) 0 K, 1 atm (d) 100 °C, 1 atm

  5. The most electronegative element is: (a) Cl (b) O (c) F (d) N

  6. Which species has trigonal planar geometry? (a) NH₃ (b) BF₃ (c) H₂O (d) CH₄

  7. The hybridisation of carbon in C₂H₂ is: (a) sp (b) sp² (c) sp³ (d) sp³d

  8. The hydrogen bond is strongest in: (a) H₂S (b) HCl (c) HF (d) HBr

  9. Quantum number that describes orbital orientation in space is: (a) n (b) l (c) mₗ (d) mₛ

  10. Bohr's atomic model could not explain: (a) Spectrum of H atom (b) Quantisation of energy (c) Spectra of multi-electron atoms (d) Stability of atoms

  11. Equilibrium constant Kp and Kc are related by: (a) Kp = Kc T (b) Kp = Kc R (c) Kp = Kc (RT)^Δn (d) Kp = Kc / RT

  12. Le Chatelier's principle does NOT apply to a change in: (a) Temperature (b) Pressure (c) Concentration (d) Catalyst

  13. Molarity is moles of solute per: (a) Litre of solvent (b) Litre of solution (c) Kg of solvent (d) Kg of solution

  14. The unit of rate constant for a first-order reaction is: (a) mol⁻¹ L s⁻¹ (b) s⁻¹ (c) mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹ (d) mol⁻² L² s⁻¹

  15. A galvanic cell converts: (a) Chemical → Electrical (b) Electrical → Chemical (c) Mechanical → Electrical (d) Heat → Chemical

  16. SI unit of rate of reaction is: (a) mol L⁻¹ (b) mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹ (c) mol s⁻¹ (d) L s⁻¹

  17. Surface tension of liquids decreases with: (a) Increase of temperature (b) Increase of pressure (c) Decrease of temperature (d) Decrease of volume

Section B — Short Response Questions (44 marks)

Attempt any ELEVEN (11) questions. Each carries 4 marks.

  1. Differentiate between empirical and molecular formula. Calculate the empirical formula of a compound containing 40% C, 6.7% H, and 53.3% O.
  2. State Avogadro's law. How does it lead to the result that 1 mole of any gas at STP occupies 22.4 L?
  3. Explain solvent extraction with one example.
  4. Sketch a labelled diagram of paper chromatography. Define Rf.
  5. State and derive Boyle's law from kinetic theory of gases.
  6. Why do real gases deviate from ideal behaviour at high pressure and low temperature? Write the van der Waals correction.
  7. Explain hydrogen bonding in water. Give one consequence (e.g. anomalous expansion of ice).
  8. Differentiate ionic and molecular crystals with two examples each.
  9. Define quantum numbers n, l, mₗ, mₛ.
  10. State Heisenberg's uncertainty principle. Write the mathematical form.
  11. Explain sp³ hybridisation in methane (CH₄) with diagram.
  12. State and explain Hess's law of constant heat summation.
  13. Define equilibrium constant Kc. Calculate Kc for N₂O₄ ⇌ 2NO₂ if at equilibrium [N₂O₄] = 0.4 M and [NO₂] = 0.2 M.
  14. State Le Chatelier's principle. Apply it to the synthesis of ammonia (Haber process).
  15. Calculate the molarity of a solution prepared by dissolving 4 g of NaOH in 250 mL solution.
  16. State Faraday's first and second laws of electrolysis.
  17. Define order and molecularity of a reaction. Give one example of each.

Section C — Long Response Questions (24 marks)

Attempt any THREE (3). Each carries 8 marks.

Q18. (a) 5 g of CaCO₃ reacts with 20 mL of 2 M HCl. Identify the limiting reagent and calculate the mass of CO₂ produced. (5) (b) Define percent yield. If the actual yield in the above reaction is 1.6 g CO₂, find the percent yield. (3)

OR

(a) Explain sp, sp², and sp³ hybridisation with one example each (acetylene, ethylene, methane). Sketch shapes and bond angles. (6) (b) Why is the bond length of C=C less than C–C? (2)

OR

(a) Define enthalpy of formation, combustion, and neutralisation. Give one example of each. (6) (b) Use Hess's law: given ΔH(C → CO₂) = −393 kJ, ΔH(CO → CO₂) = −283 kJ, find ΔH(C → CO). (2)
