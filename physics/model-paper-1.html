Section B — Short Response Questions (44 marks)

Attempt any ELEVEN (11) questions. Each carries 4 marks.

  1. Define base and derived units. Give two examples of each.
  2. Check the homogeneity of the equation v² = u² + 2as using dimensional analysis.
  3. Find the resultant of two forces of magnitude 10 N and 8 N acting at right angles to each other.
  4. State and prove the work-energy theorem.
  5. Define elastic and inelastic collision. Give one example of each.
  6. A body of mass 5 kg is moving with velocity 4 m/s. Calculate its momentum and KE.
  7. Derive the expression for the time of flight of a projectile.
  8. State Newton's law of universal gravitation. Define gravitational potential.
  9. Derive the expression for centripetal acceleration a = v²/r.
  10. Explain the principle of working of a Venturi meter.
  11. Define terminal velocity. Write down the expression derived from Stokes' law.
  12. Show that the time period of mass-spring system is T = 2π √(m/k).
  13. State the principle of superposition of waves. Define beats.
  14. Explain the formation of stationary waves in a stretched string fixed at both ends. Write the expression for the fundamental frequency.
  15. State Huygens' principle. Use it to explain the rectilinear propagation of light.
  16. Define wavefront. Differentiate spherical, plane, and cylindrical wavefronts.
  17. State the first law of thermodynamics. Apply it to an isothermal process.

Section C — Long Response Questions (24 marks)

Attempt any THREE (3) questions. Each carries 8 marks.

Q18. (a) Derive expressions for the maximum height, time of flight, and horizontal range of a projectile fired with initial velocity u at angle θ to the horizontal. (5) (b) A bullet is fired with velocity 100 m/s at 30°. Find its range, max height, and time of flight. (Take g = 10 m/s².) (3)

OR

(a) State and explain the law of conservation of momentum. Derive the velocities of two bodies after a one-dimensional elastic collision. (5) (b) A 4 kg block moving at 6 m/s collides head-on elastically with a stationary 2 kg block. Find their velocities after collision. (3)

Q19. (a) Derive the expression for the escape velocity of a body from the surface of the Earth. (5) (b) Calculate the escape velocity from a planet of mass 2M and radius R, in terms of g. (3)

OR

(a) Define SHM. Derive expressions for displacement, velocity, and acceleration of a particle executing SHM. (5) (b) A body executing SHM has a time period of 4 s and amplitude 5 cm. Find its maximum velocity and maximum acceleration. (3)

Q20. (a) Describe Young's double-slit experiment. Derive the expression for the fringe spacing. (5) (b) In a Young's double-slit experiment, slit separation d = 0.5 mm, screen distance L = 2 m, and wavelength λ = 600 nm. Find the fringe spacing. (3)

OR

(a) State the laws of reflection and refraction. Derive Snell's law using Huygens' principle. (5) (b) A diffraction grating has 5000 lines per cm. Find the angle for the second-order maximum when light of wavelength 500 nm is used. (3)

Q21. (a) Describe the working of a Carnot engine. Derive an expression for its efficiency. (5) (b) A Carnot engine works between 400 K and 250 K. Find its efficiency and the heat rejected to the sink if the heat supplied is 1000 J. (3)

OR

(a) State the second law of thermodynamics. Explain the concept of entropy with one example. (5) (b) Calculate the change in entropy when 1 kg of ice at 0 °C melts to water. (Lf = 3.36 × 10⁵ J/kg.) (3)

Q22. (a) State and derive Bernoulli's equation. (5) (b) Water flows through a horizontal pipe whose cross-section narrows from 4 cm² to 1 cm². If the speed in the wider section is 0.5 m/s, find the speed and pressure difference in the narrow section. (3)

OR

(a) Explain the Doppler effect for sound. Derive the formula when the source moves towards a stationary observer. (5) (b) A train sounding a horn of frequency 600 Hz approaches a stationary observer at 30 m/s. Find the apparent frequency. (Speed of sound = 340 m/s.) (3)
