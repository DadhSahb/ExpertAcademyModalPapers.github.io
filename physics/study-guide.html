Top guess topics — almost-certain questions

  1. Projectile motion: derivation of time of flight, max height, range (LRQ).
  2. Escape velocity derivation from energy conservation (LRQ).
  3. Banking of road — derivation of optimal angle tan θ = v² / (rg) (SRQ or part of LRQ).
  4. SHM equations + simple pendulum derivation of T = 2π √(L/g) (LRQ).
  5. Doppler effect — observer/source motion, formulas, one numerical (LRQ).
  6. Young's double-slit experiment — derivation of fringe spacing λL/d + sample numerical (LRQ).
  7. Carnot cycle — efficiency 1 − T₂/T₁, derivation + one numerical (LRQ).
  8. Bernoulli's equation application: lift on aeroplane wing OR Venturi meter (SRQ).
  9. Dimensional analysis of a given equation (SRQ).
  10. Vector resolution problem — find resultant of three forces in equilibrium (SRQ).

Common student mistakes

  • Mixing up time of flight and time to max height in projectile (factor of 2 error).
  • Sign confusion in 1st law of thermodynamics — ΔU = Q − W (W done by gas) vs ΔU = Q + W (W done on gas). KP textbook uses Q = ΔU + W.
  • Forgetting to convert units (cm to m, °C to K) — costs full marks on numericals.
  • Treating Doppler shift as always increasing frequency — they ignore the sign rule.
  • Confusing centripetal vs centrifugal — centrifugal is fictitious; never use it as the "real" force in Newton's 2nd law setup.
  • Drawing free-body diagrams sloppily in equilibrium problems — examiner penalises missing forces.

Practical tips (for the 15-mark practical paper)

The board's standard list of experiments is:

  1. Vernier callipers — dimensions of a small body, density.
  2. Screw gauge — diameter of a wire, area.
  3. Spherometer — radius of curvature.
  4. Simple pendulum — value of g.
  5. Helical spring — spring constant k.
  6. Coefficient of viscosity by Stokes' law.
  7. Resonance tube — speed of sound.
  8. Bar pendulum (compound).
  9. Young's modulus (Searle's apparatus).

Marks split: experiment 8 + viva 3 + notebook 4.

Tutor tip: have students rewrite their notebook neatly in the last week. Examiners often deduct notebook marks for messy graphs and missing precautions.

Formula sheet to memorize cold

  • Equations of motion: v = u + at, s = ut + ½at², v² = u² + 2as.
  • Projectile range: R = u² sin 2θ / g. Time of flight: T = 2u sin θ / g. Max height: H = u² sin²θ / 2g.
  • Newton's 2nd law: F = ma, momentum p = mv.
  • Work: W = F · d cos θ. KE = ½ mv², PE = mgh.
  • Escape velocity: vₑ = √(2gR).
  • Centripetal acceleration: a = v²/r = ω²r.
  • SHM: T = 2π √(m/k) (spring); T = 2π √(L/g) (pendulum).
  • Doppler: f' = f (v ± vₒ)/(v ∓ vₛ).
  • Young's fringe spacing: Δy = λL/d.
  • Carnot efficiency: η = 1 − T₂/T₁.
  • Bernoulli: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant.
