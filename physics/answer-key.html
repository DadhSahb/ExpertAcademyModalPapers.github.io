Section B — Short Response (4 marks each, attempt any 11)

B1. Base units — defined independently (e.g. metre, kilogram, second, ampere). Derived units — combinations (e.g. newton = kg·m/s², joule = kg·m²/s²). (2 + 2.)

B2. Dimensions of v² = [L²T⁻²]. u² = [L²T⁻²]. as = [LT⁻²][L] = [L²T⁻²]. All terms have the same dimension → equation is dimensionally homogeneous. (2 marks each side, 1 conclusion.)

B3. R = √(F₁² + F₂²) = √(100 + 64) = √164 ≈ 12.8 N. Direction θ = tan⁻¹(8/10) = 38.7° from the 10 N force. (2 marks magnitude, 2 marks direction.)

B4. Statement: work done by net force = change in KE. Proof from F = ma, v² = u² + 2as: W = F·s = m·a·s = m·(v² − u²)/2 = ΔKE. (2 marks statement, 2 marks derivation.)

B5. Elastic: KE conserved (e.g. collision of gas molecules). Inelastic: KE not conserved (e.g. clay ball stuck to wall). (2 + 2.)

B6. p = mv = 5 × 4 = 20 kg·m/s. KE = ½mv² = ½(5)(16) = 40 J. (2 + 2.)

B7. Vertical motion: vy = u sin θ − g t. At top, vy = 0 → t = u sin θ / g. Total time of flight = 2 u sin θ / g. (2 marks setup, 2 marks final formula.)

B8. F = G m₁m₂ / r². Gravitational potential at distance r: V = −GM/r (work done per unit mass to bring from infinity). (2 + 2.)

B9. Derivation: change in velocity in time Δt for circular motion of radius r at speed v gives |Δv| = v·Δθ. Acceleration a = Δv/Δt = v · (Δθ/Δt) = v·ω = v²/r. (2 marks geometry, 2 marks limit.)

B10. Venturi meter: a horizontal pipe with a constriction. By continuity, velocity is higher in narrow section; by Bernoulli, pressure is lower. The pressure difference is measured (manometer) and used to calculate flow rate. (2 marks principle, 2 marks application.)

B11. Terminal velocity is the constant velocity at which net force is zero (drag balances weight minus buoyancy). From Stokes' law: vₜ = 2r²(ρ − σ)g / 9η. (2 marks definition, 2 marks formula.)

B12. For mass on spring, F = −kx → ma = −kx → a = −(k/m)x, comparing with a = −ω²x gives ω = √(k/m). So T = 2π/ω = 2π √(m/k). (2 marks derivation, 2 marks final.)

B13. Superposition: when two or more waves meet, the resultant displacement = algebraic sum of individual displacements. Beats: periodic variation in loudness produced when two sound waves of slightly different frequencies superpose; beat frequency = |f₁ − f₂|. (2 + 2.)

B14. Wave reflects from both fixed ends, generating a standing wave with nodes at the ends. Fundamental: λ/2 = L → λ = 2L. Speed v = √(T/μ). So f₁ = v/(2L) = (1/2L)√(T/μ). (2 marks formation, 2 marks formula.)

B15. Huygens' principle: every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets; the new wavefront is the envelope of these wavelets. Rectilinear propagation: a small slit acts as point source, but most light continues forward; the envelope of forward-going wavelets is the next plane wavefront → light travels in straight lines. (2 marks statement, 2 marks application.)

B16. Wavefront: a surface of points oscillating in same phase. Spherical: emitted by point source. Plane: from source at infinity (parallel rays). Cylindrical: from a linear source. (1 mark definition, 1 mark each type.)

B17. First law: Q = ΔU + W (heat supplied = change in internal energy + work done by gas). For isothermal process, ΔU = 0 (T constant) → Q = W; all heat supplied is converted to work. (2 marks statement, 2 marks isothermal application.)

Section C — Long Response (8 marks each)

Q18 (main). (a) Horizontal: x = u cos θ · t. Vertical: y = u sin θ · t − ½ g t². At max height, vertical velocity = 0 → tₕ = u sin θ / g → H = u² sin²θ / 2g. Total flight time T = 2 u sin θ / g. Range R = u cos θ · T = u² sin 2θ / g. (1 mark each component, 2 marks for time of flight, 1 mark each for H and R.) (b) T = 2(100)(0.5)/10 = 10 s. H = (100)²(0.5)²/(2·10) = 125 m. R = (100)² sin 60°/10 = 1000 × 0.866 = 866 m. (1 mark each.)

Q19 (main). (a) Energy conservation: ½ m vₑ² = G M m / R → vₑ² = 2GM/R. Using g = GM/R² → vₑ = √(2gR). (3 marks setup, 2 marks final.) (b) For planet of mass 2M and radius R: vₑ' = √(2 G(2M)/R) = √2 · √(2GM/R) = √2 · vₑ_earth. In terms of g_planet (= 2g_earth): vₑ' = √(2 · 2g · R) = 2√(gR). (3 marks.)

Q20 (main). (a) Two coherent slits separated by d, screen at distance L. Path difference for nth bright fringe = nλ = d sin θ ≈ d (y/L). So bright fringes at yₙ = nλL/d. Fringe spacing Δy = yₙ₊₁ − yₙ = λL/d. (3 marks setup with diagram, 2 marks derivation.) (b) Δy = (600 × 10⁻⁹)(2)/(0.5 × 10⁻³) = 2.4 × 10⁻³ m = 2.4 mm. (3 marks.)

Q21 (main). (a) Carnot engine: 4 reversible strokes — isothermal expansion at T₁, adiabatic expansion to T₂, isothermal compression at T₂, adiabatic compression back to T₁. Heat absorbed Q₁ = nRT₁ ln(V₂/V₁), heat rejected Q₂ = nRT₂ ln(V₂/V₁) (after using adiabatic relations). Efficiency η = (Q₁ − Q₂)/Q₁ = 1 − T₂/T₁. (3 marks cycle description, 2 marks derivation.) (b) η = 1 − 250/400 = 0.375 = 37.5%. Heat rejected Q₂ = Q₁(1 − η) = 1000 × 0.625 = 625 J. (3 marks.)

Q22 (main). (a) For an ideal incompressible non-viscous fluid in steady streamline flow, sum of pressure energy + KE per unit volume + PE per unit volume is constant: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant. Derivation: apply work-energy theorem to a fluid element flowing through a section change. (3 marks statement + diagram, 2 marks derivation.) (b) By continuity: A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ → v₂ = (4)(0.5)/1 = 2 m/s. By Bernoulli (h same): P₁ − P₂ = ½ρ(v₂² − v₁²) = ½(1000)(4 − 0.25) = 1875 Pa. (3 marks.)
